About Number Systems

Number systems are mathematical notations used to represent numbers in different formats. They are the foundation of modern computing, digital systems, and everyday arithmetic.

More Info.

Here are the most common number systems:

  • Decimal (Base-10): The standard system used in daily life, consisting of digits 0–9.
  • Binary (Base-2): Used in computers and digital systems, representing numbers with only 0 and 1.
  • Octal (Base-8): A number system with digits 0–7, often used in computer applications.
  • Hexadecimal (Base-16): Includes digits 0–9 and letters A–F, used in programming and color coding.
